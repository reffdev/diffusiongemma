### The Ear-to-Shoulder Stretch

**How to do it:**
1.  **Sit up tall:** Sit at the edge of your chair with your spine straight and shoulders relaxed (don't let them hunch up toward your ears).
2.  **Drop one ear:** Slowly lower your right ear toward your right shoulder. **Do not lift your shoulder to meet your ear;** keep the shoulder heavy and low.
3.  **Add gentle tension (Optional):** For a deeper stretch, reach your left hand down toward the floor or grab the underside of your chair seat. This "anchors" your shoulder down.
4.  **Hold:** Breathe deeply and hold for **20–30 seconds**.
5.  **Switch:** Slowly bring your head back to center and repeat on the other side.

---

### Two "Bonus" Micro-Movements
If you feel "stuck" or stiff, try these two movements before the stretch to loosen the joints:

**1. Chin Tucks (The "Double Chin" Move)**
This corrects "Forward Head Posture" (tech neck).
*   Look straight ahead.
*   Without tilting your head up or down, pull your chin straight back as if you are making a double chin.
*   You should feel a stretch at the base of your skull/top of your neck.
*   Hold for 3 seconds, release, and repeat 5 times.

**2. Shoulder Blade Squeezes**
Often, neck pain is actually caused by weak upper back muscles.
*   Imagine there is a pencil between your shoulder blades.
*   Squeeze your shoulder blades together and slightly downward.
*   Hold for 5 seconds and repeat 5 times.
